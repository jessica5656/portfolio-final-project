Project: The Blanton Museum of Art Site Reimagined

Focus: Information Architecture

Research | Card Sorting | Tree Testing | Protyping

    Content Audit

  • We began by doing a content audit of the Blanton Museum of Arts current site and information architecture.

  • Findings: Mixed taxonomy, ontology and redundent, archived information overload.

    Reimagining the Information Architecture

  • After reviewing the audit and researching other similar museum sites, I came up with a ontology and taxonomy that felt logical, but I needed user data to test my theories.

  • Mural Site Map | Draft 1 | Draft 2

    Card Sorting

  • To test the Ontology and Taxonomy I was creating I also did a card sort with 6 participants. I started these card sorts live but due to the challanges of Covid-19 I moved to using Optimal Workshop.

  • Interview Raw Data
  • Card Sort Raw Data

    Site Map Draft 3

  • Using the insights from the card sort, I created a third draft of my site map.

    Tree Testing

  • I tested the third draft of my site map with a tree test

  • 17 personal participants | with a total over 100 classwide particiapants
  • Card Sort Retrospective

    Prototype | Low Fidelity

  • Low Fidelity Ideation Prototype Sketches
  • Sketch 1 | Sketch 2 | Sketch 3

    Prototype | Mid-Fidelity

  • Using all the research and date to this point, I created a mid-fi clickable prototype to use for usability testing
  • I created five tasks and tested them with three participants
  • From this point I plan to refine my proto-type to create a high-fi version with the insights of my usability testing to finish the project out.
